# Impeccable Design Context — Ontario Pay Hub

## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: Job seekers in Ontario — both actively searching (desktop, at home, focused) and passively browsing (mobile, commuting, low attention). They want to quickly assess whether a role's pay is worth their time before clicking through.

**Secondary**: HR professionals and recruiters benchmarking compensation. They need the data to be credible and scannable.

**Key insight**: The site must work in two modes — quick mobile scan (is this salary range even in my ballpark?) and focused desktop research (comparing roles, filtering, saving).

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Professional. Direct. Trustworthy.

**Tone**: Data speaks for itself. No hype, no fluff. The interface should feel like a well-designed financial tool — authoritative without being cold, helpful without being chatty. Think Bloomberg Terminal meets a well-designed consumer app.

**Emotional goal**: Users should feel *informed and in control*, not overwhelmed or uncertain.

### Aesthetic Direction
**Display font**: Bricolage Grotesque — modern geometric, slightly editorial, not corporate. Used for headings and key data labels only.

**Visual references**:
- **Raycast.com**: Dark depth with subtle blue-gray background tint, precision typography, data density without clutter, accent color used sparingly
- **Framer.com**: Bold editorial heading treatment, hierarchy through type scale not boxes, confident layout
- **Cosmos.so**: Breathing room, content-first, let the data be the design element

**Theme**: Dark primary (near-black with very subtle cool tint). Light mode supported. Ontario Red (#C8102E) as brand anchor — used sparingly at 2-3 key moments only (primary CTA, salary midpoint highlight, active filter state). Not for decorative purposes.

**Anti-references**: Government website (stiff, form-heavy), generic AI-generated dark UI (cyan accents, purple gradients, glassmorphism everywhere), IT/developer terminal aesthetic (too raw).

### Design Principles
1. **Data is the hero** — reduce UI chrome, let salary numbers be the most visually prominent element on any card
2. **Hierarchy through type, not boxes** — use font size/weight contrast to separate content; reduce reliance on borders and cards
3. **Restrained accent** — Ontario Red appears only at critical decision points (primary CTA, active filter, salary midpoint). Everywhere else: neutral palette
4. **Breathing room at scale** — generous spacing in hero and insights; tighter density acceptable in the job list where users are scanning
5. **Fast and precise** — interactions feel instant; no bounce/elastic easing; motion is functional, not decorative
